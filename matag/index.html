Model Based Systems Thinking

Systems thinking is to step back and look at all the parts of the system and not just focusing on one aspect. For example if your tv remote stopped working it’s probably because it needs new batteries not because the whole remote is broken, you don’t go out and buy a new remote each time yours dies. Digital systems work the same way, the people who are in charge of creating different digital spaces like engineers and developers have to find ways to make sure all parts of the system is working together fluidly and this requires systems thinking. A much larger form of systems thinking that many of us use in our everyday lives would aps like apple maps. Apple maps is used to direct you and help you reach your desired location in a specific time range. This app works through its large network, connecting to many different networks and sources including your data and connection, traffic updates, road maps, and more. But if one part of this system isn’t working then it can change a lot for your time of arrival to even your final destination. If you’re in a location with bad reception sometimes apple maps takes longer to update sometimes resulting in a missed turn or even loss of directions altogether .

Systems Thinking

Instead of focusing on just one part systems thinking focuses on the interactions between different parts, understanding how different parts connect and affect each other. This thought process is extremely important in the design of digital environments as well because even the smallest changes can create a completely different outcome in the system. For example if you were to forget to mix up salt and sugar when baking your cookies will taste completely different this is because each ingredient is just part of the recipe and to get the desired results you have to make sure each step is followed and executed properly. Each part of a system works together like a team to function properly, losing even one part of a working team can due damage in many different ways.

Linear thinking

Systems thinking is different than Linear thinking, linear thinking is more step by step cause and effect, this means focusing on one problem at a time. Linear thinking has been the more commonly known way of problem solving. This way of thinking can work really well when solving simpler problems that have clear and direct solution. Linear thinking fails compared to systems thinking when it comes to more complex systems and problems. It is hard to predict a digital systems outcome, when dealing with so many different components. In digital environments, systems have many parts such as software modules, user interfaces, security layers, databases, and network infrastructure. To better understand an error in the digital environment systems thinking is crucial.

Model Based Systems Engineering

Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) helps with complex systems because it uses digital models to analyze, represent and manage the system during design and development. (J. Holt) “Firstly, MBSE is not a subdivision, nor a subset, of Systems Engineering; MBSE is a complete approach to Systems Engineering and, therefore, is used for all aspects of Systems Engineering.” Systems thinking is very similar to (MBSE) but instead of only following diagrams and written documents, (MBSE) uses structured models that describe system components. Estefan (2007), (MBSE) provides a formalized approach for capturing system requirements, design structures, and interactions through models that can be analyzed and updated throughout the lifecycle of a system. Models allow the work of a system to be broken down in to a smaller form which makes it easier to target issues and correct them during the process. This make sit easier for engineers and developers to see a problem and fix it then and there instead of having to figure it out at the end and backtrack their work.

(MBSE)

Models are very important in the digital environment they help to create visuals for the engineers. Models make it possible to see how different components are going to interact and this can even help to prevent possible issues and errors early on in the design process. Holt (2021), If the Model is imagined to be a large, complex collection of information and each View is analogous to opening up a small window into that Model, then the Diagrams may be thought of as applying different filters or lenses to each window. (J. Holt) “The Model was defined earlier in this chapter as being an abstraction of the System. Think of abstraction as being a simplified representation of the System, in this case, using graphics or diagrams as the medium.” Understanding how systems function as a whole and using models to represent them helps for engineers and developers to be better prepared to analyze design and possibly even improve there work.

Systems thinking

Systems thinking and model-based systems engineering are a huge part of digital environments, this is important to know because we live in these digital environments. (J. Holt) “Whether it’s a digital system such as a website or a physical system such as a checkout line in a retail store, we form a model of how a system works, and then we apply that model to new situations in which the system is similar.” Systems thinking allows us to understand and break down the aspects of the system as whole and (MBSE) lets us create tools to organize and cleanly go through a process and its connecting parts. As digital systems grow more and more complex as we advance through the digital world using both systems thinking and (MBSE) become more important for us to understand new systems. Engineers and developers need to focus on the bigger picture and not just parts, because user experience is more important than the app, product or whatever is benign pushed. This is because users are the product, the consumers attention is what is truly needed by the developers.
