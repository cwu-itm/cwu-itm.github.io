Model Based Systems Thinking

Systems thinking is to step back and look at all the parts of the system and not just focusing on one aspect. For example if your tv remote stopped working it’s probably because it needs new batteries not because the whole remote is broken, you don’t go out and buy a new remote each time yours dies. Digital systems work the same way, the people who are in charge of creating different digital spaces like engineers and developers have to find ways to make sure all parts of the system is working together fluidly and this requires systems thinking. Instead of focusing on just one part systems thinking focuses on the interactions between different parts, understanding how different parts connect and affect each other. This thought process is extremely important in the design of digital environments as well because even the smallest changes can create a completely different outcome in the system. Systems thinking is different than Linear thinking, linear thinking is more step by step cause and effect, this means focusing on one problem at a time. Linear thinking has been the more commonly known way of problem solving. This way of thinking can work really well when solving simpler problems that have clear and direct solution. Linear thinking fails compared to systems thinking when it comes to more complex systems and problems. It is hard to predict a digital systems outcome, when dealing with so many different components. In digital environments, systems have many parts such as software modules, user interfaces, security layers, databases, and network infrastructure. To better understand an error in the digital environment systems thinking is crucial. Model Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) helps with complex systems because it uses digital models to analyze, represent and manage the system during design and development. Systems thinking is very similar to (MBSE) but instead of only following diagrams and written documents, (MBSE) uses structured models that describe system components. Estefan (2007), (MBSE) provides a formalized approach for capturing system requirements, design structures, and interactions through models that can be analyzed and updated throughout the lifecycle of a system. Models are very important in the digital environment they help to create visuals for the engineers. Models make it possible to see how different components are going to interact and this can even help to prevent possible issues and errors early on in the design process. Holt (2021), If the Model is imagined to be a large, complex collection of information and each View is analogous to opening up a small window into that Model, then the Diagrams may be thought of as applying different filters or lenses to each window.
