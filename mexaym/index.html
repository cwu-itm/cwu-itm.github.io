Module 1.1 Blog Post

Introduction

Systems thinking is essentially an ideology that stems around looking at different components of a system and the ways they interact with one another. This is used to see how they act as a whole, and the relations found rather than looking at one section of a system. Using this way of thinking allows you to see the “big picture” so you can discover patterns within a systerm and the overall behavior of a system. This matters in today’s world, so there is a more effective decision-making process when it comes to systems. As discussed before, recognizing patterns allows us to see what trends are happening.

Systems Thinking

Systems thinking is important in so many ways as there are many core components to what makes systems thinking so significant. Teague (2024) spoke about how one key characteristic of systems thinking was holistic thinking and to addition other core components would be boundaries, feedback loops, interdependence, and open and closes systems. Boundaries in systems means limits within systems so making sure it’s not too complex and can be simplified. It’s good when you can explain the system in simple terms or when you look at the foundation, it’s all simple and not too complicated as it may lead to issues. Feedback loops are closed circles of cause-and-effect, so when there is an action, there will be an effect that follows. Interdependence is when you see how a section relies on other sections to work for functionality rather than being on its own. All these core components really shine a light on why systems thinking is so special and important to thinking about when designing digital environments.

Why Systems Thinking Matters

System thinking matters so much in general as it is able to find the root problem of issues. Using the analyzation of relationships and feedback loops, you are able to detect problems in the systems. It simplifies complex issues and minimizes the difficulty in understanding the situation at hand. This is also used in UX/UI design or just designs in general as it works with multiple components and on how they work together. Using holistic thinking in all of this will have users see the entire ecosystems and not just work on the system one by one. It creates great visuals as you see it work as one and not multiple small chunks shoved together. An example is a website and with that you need a simple wireframe where you add more elements but still make sure everything on the website works together.

Systems Thinking in Practice

A great way to see systems thinking in action is looking at it in a digital environment. We can use it to visualize the power of social media as you aren’t trying to gain the attention of one user but rather multiple. Plus, in this way, you can see users as interconnected components rather than an indicisual person behind the screen. Alassad & Hussain state that currently the social media field needs to adopt systems thinking to fully understand how to optimize how a social network works. Not using this hollistic way of thinkihng makes it difficult to fully understand everything as a whole.

Real World Applications

An example of this if you pick up your phone and open a social media application is seeing influencing. Influencers have become an occupation that essentially means selling people items or services online and using social media as a platform to reach people from just about anywhere around the world. How this connects with systems thinking is that influences see a whole scope of users where they sort of adhere to a target audience but also make their content generic to relate to many people. By using a basic template by a hook and different engagement tactics, they are able to sell their product or service to the average consumer. With this I will say to be careful as AI has become quite powerful and dangerous with many people using it to quickly make a voiceover for their videos and selling fake product. Beyond that, AI had also been a tool for others to spread misinformation. An example I've seen is AI being used to steal content and mislabel it as something else or even using other user’s videos as advertisement and making fake captions.

Systems Engineering

System engineering is another subject that relates to system thinking but in simple terms it makes sure that in the design process of managing complex systems, it makes sure all the parts work together properly. So especially if you were to take a big complex project on, it makes sure to organize it so all the components work together properly. Holt talks about how systems are essential for systems engineering and how they should be established first. One major tolic is making a system hierarchy, so you can really visualize what components go where and what is more important. You can visualize this with a beehive with the queen bee being on top and the male bees at the bottom of the hierarchy chart.

Conclusion

In conclusion, it’s important to understand systems thinking when it comes to systems engineering in digital environments as it’s the foundational idea in both. It’s different from traditional ways of thinking, like working in a situation one by one, but instead of a linear approach you see things from a holistic approach.

Module 1.2 Blog Post

Introduction

The systems development life cycle (SDLC) is a framework used to develop information systems efficiently to ensure everything is smooth.

Planning

In every great project, there comes planning for the scope, risks, benefits, and more. When given a foundation for SDLC, you can give clear goals to move in the right direction. Geeksforgeeks talks about how proper planning is effective for making sure the development process aligns with the organizational goals (2025). Once everything is laid out, everything should be good to go.

Analysis

Analysis focuses on doing a thorough reading on understanding what the system requires. It means gathering any important information needed from stakeholders to review current processes. Also doing a full scope of the data collected so we can create a system that could address both user expectations and organizational challenges. This also means checking for any deficiencies in the system and making sure to address them as well.

Design

There is a project team responsible for the design of the system. This team is responsible for taking account the physical construction, hardware, operating systems, programming, databases, communications, user interfaces, and security. One task this team does is send over prototypes to stakeholders to show what the product could look like. This step of the process is also great for creating the technical blueprint. With this layer comes with the responsibility of creating the structure needed to guide how the development of the system and the implementation activities will go.

Development

The development stage is where we will see some actions take place with developers using coding in the system. They’ll communicate with the designers to see what specifications are needed as well as implementing features, creating databases, and of course writing their code. It also created an initial internal testing so we can make sure the system works properly and up to date. The development crew works alongside the design team to make sure the system is functional.

Testing

The testing stage is when they use tests to system tests to confirm if the system works properly. Awati spoke about measures the testing stage takes with making sure the customer satisfaction expectations are up to par (2024). Also, the project team will make adjustments to the system if needed when they get the results of the testing back. This can be a wide range of issues that need to be addressed, such as bugs, defects, security gaps, and other possible vulnerabilities. There are also tests that are performed by the quality assurance (QA) teams, so they can include systems integration, user acceptance testing (UAT), and system testing. This stage is crucial to ensure that the system is free of errors, and everything always functions correctly. We talk about testing during systems but also making sure to do tests before the deployment stage is also extremely important to fix any issues beforehand.

Implementation

The implementation phase is the key action to send off the system and to deploy the developed system to live in a live environment. Once the new system is fresh into a production environment and integrated with other systems, users can be trained for it. The system can be deployed in several ways, such as system installation, migrating data, training users, and configurating infrastructure. This really helps make sure that when the new system is deployed, the whole transition will be smooth, so there are minimal disruptions. The procedure is that they replace the old system with the new one but in some cases it’s more cost-effective to just completely shut down the old system to then replace it and implement the new system. Then all users would be trained for this new system and to make sure everything is in working order.

Maintenance

After all these steps, it’s then time to do maintenance and to keep up with the system to make sure that there are no complications. It involves upgrading and changing the system once it's all in place. The organization maintains it so they may replace any hardware or software to keep up with users. This step also fixes bugs, performance enhancements, security patches, and responding to user feedback. Making sure to have proper maintenance with the system is highly important to ensure the system remains efficient, secure, and adaptable for future business needs.

Conclusion

Overall, there are different teams that all come together to make a system thrive. To ensure everything goes smoothly, we can ensure that we give out key roles and responsibility to team members so that the framework and be as efficient as process. One thing we do is that it's time to take action on this process to satisfy customers. The SDLC makes sure that the risk factor is low if all steps are properly followed so that when it comes to deploying the new system, the possibility of failure is low.
