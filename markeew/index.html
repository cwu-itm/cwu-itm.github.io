What is Systems Thinking

Systems thinking is a proplem solving approch to look at how parts of the sytstem come togther to make a whole. The focues of systems thinking is to understand how the seperate parts interact with eachother to make the whole system work. A use of Sytsems thinking in the real world is applying it to safty as descriped in the book engineering a safer world by Nancy G. Leveson. Leveson describes how implementing the modern ideas of systems thinking and systems theory into safty analyses and risk managment to create a new safty system that that is better sutied to handel the modern age of inovation. This new system is better suited to handle the growing relationship between human and automated working enviormants and the new kinds of hazadrs that are posible with further advances in science and manufactioring technologies.(Leveson, 2011) Just to name one example of a application of systems thinking.

How Systems Thinking differs From Triditional Ways of Thinking

System thinking differs for tridtional thinking becasue tradtional thinking looks at a system in individual parts and thinks about how they form a linear or cause and effect relationship, where as system thinking looks at the whole of the system. Tradtional thinking makes it hard to understand complex systems becasue of reductionisum mindset to look at parts in isolation from eachother.

Thinking in Life Cycle

Starting with the definition of a system life cycle, it is the development of a system through six stages, Conception, Development, Production, Utilization, Support, and Retirement. (Holt, 2021) The conception stage is concered with identifiying the needs of the systems and work on stackholder analyses. The development stage works on finding possiple solutions to the proplem and work on a prototype to test the solution. The production stage takes the solution and develops and test the actual system to make sure that it is built properly. The utilization stage shows how users and trained to use it and how the system performes whem in use. The support stage helps to solve problems that come up during usage of the system. The last stage is retierment which is when the system is decommissioned and disposed of at the end of its life cycle.

Looking at the differances between the linier and nonliner life cycle models take a much more structured path that prioritises effectiveness and predictability. Nonliner models are more dynamic, flexable, and adaptable allowing for complex problems to be solved and mutiple task to be worked on a the same time. The nonliner models are better sutied for modern day system development and proplem solving.
